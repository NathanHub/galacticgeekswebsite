UNREAL Game Jam 2021

Hosted by Tanmay Sharma, Arastu Tamboli, Sai Hemanth · #IACGGamejam2021
Overview

IACG's first ever Game Jam !

This Game Jam will be a 72-hours jam, starting on the 27th of October and concluding on the 30th of October. 

What is Unreal Game Jam?

This Game Jam is about developing your own concepts and ideas into a playable game made using Unreal Engine only. Although, it should be based on the themes given at the start of the competetion.

When & Where

Offline, here at IACG Madhapur!

  • 27th Oct - Jam Starts
  • 30th Oct - Jam Ends
  • 30th Oct - 1st Nov - Jury Evaluation
  • 2nd Nov - Winners announced for Unreal Game jam 2021


Why Participate?

There are a number of reasons why you should participate in the Unreal Game Jam:

  • Create a working prototype (proof of concept) of your game in a focused sprint, in limited time.
  • Get judged by an awesome jury and get real constructive feedback.
  • Showcase & push your game development skills and level up!
  • Team up with your awesome classmates.

Format this year?

We'll be going ahead with the good ol' relaxed 72hour competition format! The compo will start at 3PM on the 27th of October 2021 and end on the 30th of October 2021 at 3PM! You will have 72 hours to create an amazing game!

Prizes

The winners will be getting something amazing and that'll be a surprise.

The Theme

  • Let there be chaos
  • Stronger Together
  • Running Out of Power

You can interpret them in anyway you want. You need to use at least one of them, but you can combine any of them together and use it also.

Judging Criteria

This year, (once) we've managed to get some of the industry experts to be the Jury and they are going to play all the games and judge them on the following criteria:

  • Overall Experience - How was the overall experience of the game?
  • Relevance to Theme - How relevant was the theme of the game?
  • Gameplay - How engaging/fun was the gameplay? Was it interesting, meaningful etc? 
  • Innovation - Did the game do something new that you may have not seen before?
  • Aesthetics - How aesthetically pleasing was the experience - narrative, visual, audio etc?
